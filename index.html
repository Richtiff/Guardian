BEFORE WE START
Guardian will build your position statement using everything it knows about your case — your diary entries, uploaded documents, message history, dismissed findings, and case profile.
The stronger your case file, the stronger your statement. Make sure you have uploaded your key documents before continuing.
Be specific. Guardian will make sure every section of your statement builds towards this.
Tap a hearing type to see what it means and what your statement needs to achieve.
First hearing — FHDRA
Your first appearance in court. Be prepared — this hearing rarely goes the way parents hope. The judge is not here to make decisions today. They are finding out what the case is about and deciding what happens next. Most FHDRA hearings end with another hearing being listed and a Cafcass section 7 report being ordered. That is normal. Do not be disheartened.
introduce yourself calmly and make a good first impression. State your position clearly. Address the Cafcass safeguarding letter directly. Make a reasonable interim proposal for contact while the case continues. Save your strongest arguments for the next hearing — today is about showing the court you are reasonable, child-focused, and not a risk. Plant the seeds. The judge will remember how you came across today.
Contested hearing — dispute resolution
Both sides are still disagreeing and the court is trying to help you reach an agreement before a final hearing. Cafcass may have filed a section 7 report.
address every allegation directly, reference any Cafcass findings that support your position, show the court you have tried to resolve this, and make a clear specific proposal for contact.
Enforcement hearing — C79
There is already a court order in place but it is not being followed. You are asking the court to enforce it. This is serious — a breach of a court order can result in penalties including unpaid work or in severe cases committal.
document every specific breach with dates and times, show the impact on your child, demonstrate you have tried to resolve it without court intervention, and ask the court to enforce the order and consider enforcement measures.
Review hearing
The court ordered a review to check how things are going since the last order or directions. This is your chance to tell the court what has worked, what has not, and what needs to change.
report honestly on what has happened since the last hearing, highlight any positives, address any issues, and make proposals for what should happen going forward.
Final hearing
This is the most important hearing. The judge will make a final decision that will become a sealed court order. Both sides will give evidence. This is your one opportunity to put your full case before the court.
be comprehensive, address every allegation with evidence, put your child's welfare at the centre of every section, make a detailed specific proposal for what you want the court to order, and explain clearly why that is in your child's best interests.
